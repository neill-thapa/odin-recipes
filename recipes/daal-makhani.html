Recipe of Daal Makhani

Description

Daal Makhani is a rich, creamy lentil dish from North India, made with whole black lentils and kidney beans slow-cooked with butter, cream, and spices. Its silky texture and deep, smoky flavor make it a favorite for festive occasions and everyday meals alike. Serve with naan, roti, or steamed rice for a complete experience.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole black lentils (sabut urad dal)
  • ¼ cup kidney beans (rajma)
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • ¼ cup cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish

Steps

  1. Soak black lentils and kidney beans overnight. Drain and pressure cook with 3 cups water and salt until soft.
  2. Heat butter in a pan, add cumin seeds, onions, and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies, cook for 2 minutes.
  4. Mix in tomato puree, turmeric, chili powder, and cook until oil separates.
  5. Add cooked lentils, simmer on low heat for 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Stir in cream and garam masala. Cook for 5 more minutes.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve hot.
